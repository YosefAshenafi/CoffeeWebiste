SHORT ETHIOPIAN COFFEE HISTORY

Coffee arabica is an Ethiopian indigenous plant. Ethiopia was the only country in the world where coffee was consumed 1100 years ago. The discovery of Ethiopian coffee brings up the story of Khald. According to the legend, Khald's goats tasted the coffee leafs berry, cheered, and felt full of hapiness. Khald then began to investigate why his goats behavior changed. He then tasted the berry for himself. In turn, his mind was awakened and he began to rejoice. Recognizing the powerful stimulus, Khald brought some of coffee berries to his wife. When she tasted it and she too was enlightened and praised him, exclaiming, "This is from heaven."

Yemen was the first destination for coffee when it crossed the border and spread to other continents and countries. Arab caravan merchants came to Ethiopia for trade in 520 A.D and took coffee beans to their homeland and began to spread coffee in Yemen.

500 years ago the Europeans started purchasing coffee from Arab and it was given the scientific name "Coffee Arabica"

Coffee is the second most consumed liquid in the world right now, only behind water. It is also the world's second largest commodity - only behind oil.
